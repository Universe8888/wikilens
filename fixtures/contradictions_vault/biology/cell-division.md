# Cell Division

## Mitosis

Mitosis is the process by which a eukaryotic cell divides to produce two genetically identical daughter cells. It consists of four main phases: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase, followed by cytokinesis.

## Meiosis

Meiosis is the specialized form of cell division that produces four haploid daughter cells, each with half the chromosome number of the parent cell. It is used in sexual reproduction to produce gametes.

## DNA Replication

Before cell division, DNA replication occurs during the S phase of the cell cycle. Each DNA strand serves as a template, resulting in two identical double-stranded DNA molecules.

## Chromosomes in Humans

Human somatic cells contain 46 chromosomes (23 pairs). After meiosis, gametes contain 23 chromosomes — one from each pair.
