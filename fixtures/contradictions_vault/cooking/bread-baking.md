# Bread Baking

## Yeast Fermentation

Yeast ferments sugars in flour to produce carbon dioxide and ethanol. The carbon dioxide gas is trapped by gluten, causing the dough to rise. Most of the ethanol evaporates during baking.

## Gluten Development

Gluten forms when glutenin and gliadin proteins in wheat flour combine with water and are worked mechanically. Adequate gluten development gives bread its chewy, elastic structure.

## Baking Temperature

Most standard bread loaves bake at 190–220°C (375–430°F). Higher temperatures produce a crispier crust through Maillard browning; lower temperatures result in a softer crust.

## Sourdough Starter

A sourdough starter is a culture of wild yeast and lactic acid bacteria maintained in a flour-water mixture. The bacteria produce lactic and acetic acids that give sourdough its characteristic tang.
