# Coffee Brewing Methods

## Espresso

Espresso is made by forcing hot water at high pressure (9 bar) through finely-ground coffee. The result is a concentrated shot with a layer of crema on top. Brew time is typically 25–30 seconds.

## French Press

French press coffee is made by steeping coarsely ground coffee in hot water for 3–4 minutes, then pressing a metal mesh plunger to separate grounds from liquid. It produces a full-bodied cup with more oils than filtered methods.

## Pour-Over

Pour-over brewing involves slowly pouring hot water over coffee grounds in a filter. The controlled pour rate and bloom step (pre-wetting the grounds) allow precise extraction and a clean, bright cup.
