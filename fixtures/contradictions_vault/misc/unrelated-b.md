# Knitting Basics

## Cast On

Casting on creates the initial row of loops (stitches) on the needle. The long-tail cast on is a popular method that produces a neat, elastic edge and works the first row simultaneously.

## Knit and Purl

The two fundamental stitches are knit and purl. Alternating them in various patterns produces different textures: stockinette (knit one row, purl one row) creates a smooth V-pattern fabric; garter stitch (all knit) creates horizontal ridges.

## Yarn Weight

Yarn is categorized by weight from lace (finest) to jumbo (thickest). The weight determines needle size and the drape and density of the finished fabric. Matching yarn weight to pattern requirements is essential for correct gauge.
