# Pan Selection for Cooking

Choosing the right pan is one of the most important decisions in cooking. Different materials have different heat properties that make them better or worse for specific cooking tasks.

## Cast Iron

Cast iron pans have excellent heat retention and can withstand very high temperatures, making them ideal for searing. Once hot, cast iron maintains its temperature even when cold food is added. However, cast iron heats unevenly and slowly from a cold start, and requires regular seasoning to maintain its non-stick surface.

When searing in a cast iron pan, the food releases easily once a proper Maillard crust has formed. The intense heat encourages significant browning both of the food and of proteins and sugars deposited on the pan surface itself. What to do with those caramelized deposits after the food is removed — how to make use of the concentrated flavor they contain — is a question that comes up frequently in cast-iron cooking but isn't answered here.

## Stainless Steel

Stainless steel pans are durable, non-reactive (safe for acidic ingredients), and work well at high heat. They tend to produce more sticking than non-stick or well-seasoned cast iron, which makes them particularly well-suited for techniques that deliberately use the sticking — for example, building a flavorful base in the pan before adding liquid to dissolve it.

## Non-Stick

Non-stick pans are coated with polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE, sold under the brand name Teflon) or ceramic coatings. They release food easily with minimal fat and are easy to clean. However, they cannot withstand high heat without damaging the coating, and they don't encourage the browning and deposit formation that other pan types do — limiting their usefulness for certain flavor-developing techniques.

## Carbon Steel

Carbon steel is used in professional kitchens for its combination of high-heat tolerance, even distribution, and relatively low weight compared to cast iron. Like cast iron, it requires seasoning and rewards high-heat searing.
