# Acids and Bases

Acids and bases are two of the oldest classifications in chemistry. Several
definitions exist, each useful for different contexts.

## Brønsted-Lowry Definition

An acid is a proton (H+) donor; a base is a proton acceptor. Under this
definition, [[water]] can act as either — donating a proton to become OH-,
or accepting one to become H3O+. This dual behavior is called amphoteric.

## pH Scale

pH measures the concentration of H+ ions in solution, on a logarithmic
scale from 0 to 14:

- pH 7: neutral (pure water at 25°C)
- pH < 7: acidic (lemon juice ~2, stomach acid ~1.5)
- pH > 7: basic (baking soda ~9, household ammonia ~11)

Each pH unit is a 10× change in H+ concentration.

## Strong vs Weak

A strong acid fully dissociates in water — HCl donates essentially all its
protons. A weak acid only partially dissociates — acetic acid in vinegar
keeps most of its H+ bound. Same distinction for bases.

## Buffers

A buffer is a solution that resists pH changes when small amounts of acid
or base are added. Buffers are crucial in biology — blood is buffered
around pH 7.4, and even small deviations can be fatal.
