# Atoms

Atoms are the basic units of ordinary matter. Everything you can touch is made
of them — roughly 10^27 atoms in a human body, to sketch the scale.

## Structure

An atom has a dense nucleus at its center, containing positively charged
protons and electrically neutral neutrons. Electrons — far lighter and
negatively charged — occupy a cloud of probability around the nucleus.

The number of protons (the atomic number) determines the element. Carbon has
6, oxygen has 8, iron has 26. Neutron count can vary, producing isotopes of
the same element.

## Electron Shells and Bonding

Electrons populate discrete energy levels, or shells. The outermost shell —
the valence shell — controls how an atom interacts with other atoms. Atoms
bond by sharing, donating, or accepting valence electrons, forming the
[[chemical-bonds]] that hold molecules together.

## Empty Mostly

An atom is overwhelmingly empty space. If the nucleus were a marble at the
center of a football field, the electrons would be at the edges of the
stadium. "Solid" matter is an illusion of electromagnetic forces between
electron clouds.
