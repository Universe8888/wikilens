# Cells

The cell is the basic structural and functional unit of all known living
organisms. Cells range from the simplest prokaryotes (bacteria, archaea) —
which lack membrane-bound nuclei — to the complex eukaryotic cells that make
up plants, animals, and fungi.

## Key Organelles in Eukaryotes

- **Nucleus**: houses [[dna]] and coordinates gene expression.
- **[[mitochondria]]**: produce most of the cell's ATP.
- **Chloroplasts** (plants only): site of [[photosynthesis]].
- **Endoplasmic reticulum**: protein and lipid synthesis.
- **Golgi apparatus**: modifies and packages proteins for export.
- **Lysosomes**: break down waste and worn-out parts.

## Cell Membrane

The plasma membrane is a phospholipid bilayer embedded with proteins. It
controls what enters and leaves the cell through diffusion, osmosis, and
active transport. Cholesterol molecules help regulate membrane fluidity in
animal cells.

## Cell Division

Somatic cells divide via mitosis, producing two genetically identical
daughter cells. Gametes (sperm and eggs) are produced via meiosis, which
halves the chromosome number and generates genetic variation through
recombination.
