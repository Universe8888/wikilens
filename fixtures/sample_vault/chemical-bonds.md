# Chemical Bonds

Chemical bonds are the forces that hold [[atoms]] together in molecules and
compounds. There are a handful of main types, each with different strengths
and behaviors.

## Ionic Bonds

One atom transfers an electron to another. The resulting ions (one positive,
one negative) attract each other electrostatically. Table salt — NaCl — is
the textbook example: sodium gives an electron to chlorine. Ionic compounds
tend to have high melting points and dissolve well in [[water]].

## Covalent Bonds

Atoms share electrons. This is the dominant bond type in organic molecules.
A covalent bond can be single, double, or triple, depending on how many
electron pairs are shared. Most biological molecules — proteins, DNA,
carbohydrates — are built from covalent frameworks.

## Hydrogen Bonds

A weaker interaction where a hydrogen atom covalently bonded to a very
electronegative atom (O, N, F) is attracted to another electronegative atom
nearby. Hydrogen bonds are crucial to water's properties and to DNA's
double helix structure.

## Van der Waals Forces

The weakest, arising from transient dipoles as electron clouds shift. Yet
they're strong enough in aggregate to let geckos walk on ceilings.
