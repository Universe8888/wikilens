# Knife Skills

Knife work is the most leveraged skill in the kitchen. Not because
perfect brunoise makes food taste better, but because confident cutting
is fast, safe, and makes cooking feel like less of a chore.

## The Grip

Pinch the blade just forward of the bolster between thumb and index
finger. Wrap the other three fingers around the handle. This gives
control at the tip, where you actually cut. Holding only the handle is
a beginner mistake — you end up levering from the wrong pivot.

## The Claw

The hand not holding the knife steadies the food in a loose claw:
fingertips curled under, knuckles forward. The flat side of the blade
rests against the knuckles and rides them as you cut. Done right, the
edge cannot reach your fingertips. Done wrong, it absolutely can.

## Cuts by Dimension

- **Brunoise**: 1–2 mm cubes. Garnish.
- **Small dice**: ~6 mm. Mirepoix, salsas.
- **Medium dice**: ~12 mm. Stews, roasts.
- **Julienne**: matchsticks, 2×2×40 mm. Stir-fries, salads.
- **Chiffonade**: thin ribbons of leafy herbs or greens.

## Maintenance

A dull knife is more dangerous than a sharp one — it slips and requires
more force. Hone with a steel before each cooking session; sharpen on
stones every few months. Never put good knives in the dishwasher.
