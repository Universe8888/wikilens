# Light

Light is electromagnetic radiation in the wavelength range the human eye
can detect — roughly 380 to 750 nanometers. More broadly, "light" often
refers to the entire electromagnetic spectrum.

## Wave-Particle Duality

Light behaves as both a wave and a particle, depending on how you measure
it. The double-slit experiment famously shows interference patterns
(wave behavior) even when single photons are fired one at a time.

As a wave: light has wavelength, frequency, amplitude, and polarization.
As a particle: a photon carries a discrete quantum of energy, E = hf,
where h is Planck's constant and f is frequency.

## Speed

In vacuum, light travels at exactly 299,792,458 m/s — a value so central
that [[relativity]] elevates it to a fundamental constant of nature. In
other media (water, glass, air), light slows down, producing refraction.

## Colors

Visible light splits across wavelengths from violet (short) to red (long).
Newton demonstrated this with a prism, showing that "white" light is
actually a mixture. Objects appear colored because they absorb some
wavelengths and reflect others — a red apple reflects red wavelengths.

## Beyond Visible

The electromagnetic spectrum extends well beyond what we can see:
radio, microwaves, infrared, ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays. They
all travel at light speed and differ only in wavelength and energy.
