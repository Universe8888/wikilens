# Water

Water — H2O — is arguably the strangest common substance on Earth. Two
hydrogen atoms covalently bonded to one oxygen, bent at about 104.5°, create
a polar molecule with remarkable properties.

## Unusual Properties

- **High specific heat**: water absorbs a lot of energy per degree of
  temperature change. This stabilizes Earth's climate.
- **Solid less dense than liquid**: ice floats. Because of [[chemical-bonds]]
  (specifically hydrogen bonding), water molecules lock into a crystal
  lattice with more empty space than the liquid form.
- **Universal solvent**: water dissolves more substances than any other
  common liquid, thanks to its polarity.
- **High surface tension**: hydrogen bonds pull water molecules toward each
  other, producing the skin effect you see on a droplet.

## Biological Role

Life as we know it depends on liquid water. Water is the medium for most
biochemistry — in [[cells]], in blood, in tears, in sap. Every known
cellular process happens in aqueous solution.

## Why Ice Floats

When water freezes, hydrogen bonds fix the molecules in a hexagonal
arrangement that holds them slightly farther apart than in the liquid phase.
The solid is therefore about 9% less dense. This quirk is why lakes freeze
top-down and fish survive winter.
